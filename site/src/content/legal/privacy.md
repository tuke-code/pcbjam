## 1. A quick summary

This summary is here to help you understand our practices at a glance. It is **not a substitute** for the full policy below, which is what legally applies.

- **Who we are.** PCBJam is a browser-based PCB-design application (built on the open-source KiCad EDA toolchain) operated by Emergence-Engineering Kft. ("PCBJam", "we", "us"). We are the **data controller** for the personal data described here.
- **What we collect.** The email address you give us when you join the waitlist, account details, the projects and design files you create or upload, limited technical and usage data, your communications with us, and — if you buy a paid plan — limited transaction data from our payment provider.
- **Your design files.** Your projects are **private by default.** We process them on our servers only to run the service you ask for (storage, rendering, design-rule checks, compiling, exports, and the collaboration/sharing you choose to enable). We do not sell them and we do not use their contents to advertise to you.
- **Analytics.** We use **Plausible Analytics, which is cookieless and does not track you across other websites.** It does not build an advertising profile of you.
- **Payments** are handled by **Paddle**, which acts as the "Merchant of Record" and an **independent data controller** for your payment information (see §8).
- **Your rights.** You can access, correct, export, or delete your data, object to certain processing, and withdraw consent at any time. EU/UK users can complain to a data-protection regulator; California users have specific rights under the CCPA (see §13).
- **Contact.** contact@emergence-engineering.com (or hello@pcbjam.com).

## 6. Analytics — and why we don't track you

We use **Plausible Analytics** (Plausible Insights OÜ, an EU company) to understand how PCBJam is used so we can improve it. According to Plausible, this product is **cookieless** and privacy-focused:

- it does **not** use cookies or any other device storage; instead, visitors are counted using a **hash generated from the incoming request**, with a salt that is **rotated and deleted every 24 hours**, so no persistent identifier exists;
- it does **not** collect identifiers that **track or cross-check you across different applications or websites**, and your **raw IP address is not stored**;
- the data is **anonymous and aggregated** — page views, approximate location, device/OS/browser type, and referrer — and is **stored on servers in the EU**.

Because this analytics does **not** involve cross-context behavioural advertising or cross-site tracking, **we do not consider it a "sale" or "sharing" of personal information** under California law (see §13). Plausible acts as our **processor** for this analytics. See Plausible's [data policy](https://plausible.io/data-policy) and [Data Processing Agreement](https://plausible.io/dpa).

## 8. Payments — Paddle (Merchant of Record)

When you buy a paid PCBJam plan, the seller of record is **Paddle**, our payment provider, acting as the **"Merchant of Record."** This means:

- **You contract with Paddle for the transaction.** Paddle resells our software and handles billing, payment processing, and sales-tax/VAT collection and remittance.
- For your **payment data, Paddle acts as an independent data controller** — not merely our processor. Paddle collects and processes your payment details under **its own privacy notice**, and shares limited transaction data back to us (such as your name, country, plan, billing status, and card type/last four digits) so we can provide the service and keep our records. **PCBJam never receives or stores your full payment-card number.** Card data is handled by Paddle and its PCI-DSS-compliant processors.
- Please read **[Paddle's Buyer Privacy Notice](https://www.paddle.com/legal/privacy)** to understand how Paddle handles your payment data, including your rights against Paddle directly.

## 10. Public sharing and user-generated content

PCBJam lets you **publish projects and share them** with others (for example via a public project or a share link). Please remember:

- **Anything you make public can be viewed, copied, downloaded, and re-shared by others, and may be indexed by search engines.** Once content is public, we cannot control how others use it, and removing it later may not erase copies others have already made.
- **You decide what to share.** Projects are private by default; you choose if and when to publish or invite collaborators. You can change a project's visibility or stop sharing at any time in your settings.
- **You are responsible for the personal data you include** in content you publish or share (for example, names in a title block, or comments). Do not publish personal data about others without a lawful basis.
- For content you share, you are typically the controller of the personal data you choose to include; we process it as part of providing the sharing feature you requested.

## 13. Your privacy rights

### 13.1 Everyone / EU & UK users (GDPR)

Subject to the conditions in the law, you have the right to:

- **Access** a copy of your personal data (Art. 15);
- **Rectification** — correct inaccurate or incomplete data (Art. 16);
- **Erasure** — delete your data ("right to be forgotten") (Art. 17);
- **Restriction** of processing (Art. 18);
- **Data portability** — receive your data in a portable format, or have it sent to another provider (Art. 20);
- **Object** to processing based on our legitimate interests, and to direct marketing at any time (Art. 21);
- **Withdraw consent** at any time, where we rely on consent (Art. 7(3)); and
- not be subject to a decision based **solely on automated processing** that produces legal or similarly significant effects (Art. 22). **We do not use such automated decision-making.**

To exercise any right, email **contact@emergence-engineering.com**. We will respond within one month (extendable by two further months for complex requests). We will not charge you or treat you differently for exercising your rights.

**Right to complain.** If you think we have mishandled your data, you can lodge a complaint with your local supervisory authority. Our lead authority is **the Hungarian National Authority for Data Protection and Freedom of Information (NAIH — Nemzeti Adatvédelmi és Információszabadság Hatóság), naih.hu**. If you are in the United Kingdom, you can also complain to the **Information Commissioner's Office (ICO, ico.org.uk)**. We'd appreciate the chance to address your concern first.

## 14. Children's privacy

PCBJam is a general-audience engineering tool and is **not directed to children.** You must be at least **16** years old to create an account. (Under the GDPR, the "digital age of consent" is between 13 and 16 depending on your country — in Hungary, where we are established, it is 16, and we apply 16 everywhere. If you are below that age, a parent or guardian must create and manage the account on your behalf and is responsible for your use.)

We do not knowingly collect personal data from children under **13** (the US COPPA threshold), and we do not operate a parental-consent mechanism for under-13s — the service is simply not directed at them. **We do not require visitors to state their age**, but if we become aware that we hold personal data of a child under 13 (or under the applicable digital age of consent), we will delete it promptly. If you believe a child has provided us personal data, contact **contact@emergence-engineering.com** and we will remove it.

## 15. How we protect your data

We implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect your data, taking into account the state of the art, the costs, and the risks involved (Article 32 GDPR). These include encryption of data **in transit** (and at rest where supported by our providers), access controls and least-privilege, authentication safeguards, logging, and regular review of our security practices.

No method of transmission or storage is completely secure, so we cannot guarantee absolute security. If a personal-data breach occurs that is likely to result in a risk to your rights, we will **notify the competent supervisory authority without undue delay and, where feasible, within 72 hours** (Article 33 GDPR), and we will inform affected users where the breach is likely to result in a **high risk** to them (Article 34 GDPR).
